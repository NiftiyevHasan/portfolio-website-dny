CAR-GO
Case Study

Car-Go app is a creative industry startup that allows people to ship their needs. The product I am working on is an application that includes a package delivery service. The purpose of writing this application is that the packages that people want to deliver to local places are sent to the address on the road for a certain fee, if it is on the road of a driver going in the same direction with his car. To accelerate transportation of the package they carry while driving in the same direction.

Problem Statement

The Car-Go app, a startup in the creative industry, offers a unique service for shipping needs by leveraging a package delivery application. The primary challenge is to optimize the transportation of packages to local destinations by matching them with drivers traveling in the same direction. The problem to address is how to efficiently and effectively connect senders and drivers to expedite the delivery process while ensuring the safety and security of the transported packages.

I used a 5-stage Design Thinking model proposed by the Hasso Plattner Institute of Design at Stanford (the d. school"). The stages of this Design Thinking process are as follows:1. Empathise, 2. Define, 3. Ideate, 4. Prototype, 5. Test.

Solution

User Reviews and Ratings: Introduce a review and rating system for both senders and drivers. This can help build trust and accountability within the community.

In-app Messaging: Create a secure in-app messaging system that allows senders and drivers to communicate without revealing personal contact information. This ensures privacy and safety.

Dynamic Pricing: Implement a flexible pricing model that adjusts based on package size, weight, distance, and demand. This encourages more drivers to participate and offers competitive pricing for senders.

Insurance Options: Provide insurance options for senders to protect their packages during transportation, further enhancing trust and security.

Community Guidelines: Establish clear community guidelines and codes of conduct for both senders and drivers to ensure responsible and ethical interactions.

Safety Education: Offer safety education materials and resources to both senders and drivers, promoting best practices for package handling and transportation.

User-Friendly App Design: Continuously improve the user interface and experience to make the app easy to use and navigate for all users.

Customer Support: Provide responsive and accessible customer support to address any issues or concerns promptly.

Sustainability Initiatives: Promote sustainability by encouraging drivers to adopt eco-friendly practices, such as carpooling or using electric vehicles when possible.

User Flow

After numerous testing and feedback I altered the user flow of the app, so it would be a better experience for all users. Due to the short time frame for the project, I decided to focus my efforts on improving the recording process..

Flow 1 : Account

Scenario 1 : User wants to log in or register.

Scenario 2: Existing user accidentally deleted the app and would like to log in with the reset app.

Flow 2 : Support

Scenario 1 : Encounter problems while trying to ship or deliver something.

Scenario 2 : Encounter problems in my activity page such as bidding, or except bidding.

Scenario 3 : Encounter problems in account page.

Flow 3 : Shipment Order

Scenario 1 : Existing user wants to ship item or delivery item.

Scenario 2 : Existing user wants to bid and complete process.

Scenario 3 : New user wants to post item. Scenario 4 : New user wants to deliver and bid item
